Insights & Visualisations

Phenology Stage Percentage Spread:

This pie chart visually represents the distribution of different phenological stages of the Aloe Ferox. This chart provides insights into the frequency of different phenological stages within the observed sample.

Phenology Stage By Month

The horizontal axis shows the months in the year. The vertical axis represents the number of observations recorded for each phenological stage of the Aloe Ferox. The chart visually shows when specific stages are most likely to occur during the year.

Heat Map

This heat map is an overlay on a map of the southern part of South Africa, highlighting observed locations of the Aloe Ferox plant.

Scatter Plot By Province

This scatter plot diagram displays data points across the provinces the Aloe Ferox has been observed in. The horizontal axis represents days in the month. The vertical axis has the number of times the Aloe Ferox was observed on that day.

Multiple Observations Table

Province: Indicates the province where observations were made.
County: Specifies the county or specific location within the province.
First Observed Date: The date when the observation began.
Last Observed Date: The date when the observation ended.
Stage Duration (days): The duration of the observed stage (measured in days).
Peak Stage Date: The specific date and time when the stage reached its peak.

Phenology Distributon Exploration

The table is organized by Province and County. It includes columns for different stages of plant development: Bud, Flower, Fruit, and No Evidence. There's also a Total column at the end.
