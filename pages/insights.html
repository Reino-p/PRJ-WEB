Insights & Visualisations

Phenology Stage Percentage Spread:

The pie chart titled "Phenology Stage Percentage Spread" shows the distribution of different phenology stages for Aloe Ferox.

The majority of the observations are in the Bud stage, followed by Flower, Fruit, and No Evidence. This suggests that the Aloe ferox plant is most commonly observed in the budding stage.

Phenology Stage By Month

A line graph showing the number of observations for each phenology stage by month. The graph shows clear seasonal trends in the phenology stages:
  • Budding peaks in July
  • Flowering peaks in August
  • Fruiting peaks in September
  • There is a noticeable increase in phenological activities from April to September, indicating a seasonal pattern

Heat Map

Displays the distribution and intensity of the different phenology stages across South Africa.

The heatmaps indicate that the phenological activities (fruiting, flowering, and budding) are concentrated in specific regions of South Africa.
The intensity of these activities varies, with the coastal region showing higher concentrations

Scatter Plot By Province

This scatter plot diagram displays data points across the provinces the Aloe Ferox has been observed in. The horizontal axis represents days in the month. The vertical axis has the number of times the Aloe Ferox was observed on that day.

The scatter plot offers valuable insights into the temporal and regional distribution of observations across the three provinces. The clustering of data points and the presence of significant outliers can help identify patterns and anomalies, aiding in further analysis and decision-making

Multiple Observations Table

  • Province: Indicates the province where observations were made.
  • County: Specifies the county or specific location within the province.
  • First Observed Date: The date when the observation began.
  • Last Observed Date: The date when the observation ended.
  • Stage Duration (days): The duration of the observed stage (measured in days).
  • Peak Stage Date: The specific date and time when the stage reached its peak.
